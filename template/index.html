How Our Project Works?

In our proposed work, we aim to detect and classify the plant diseases using the concept of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). Convolutional Neural Networks is a class of artificial neural network that works on the concept of hidden layers and is most widely used to in image processing and recognition. The basic framework or flow of our project consists of 6 steps, namely: Image Acquisition, Image Pre-processing, Image Segmentation, Feature Extraction, Classification and Result

Image Acquisition

This is the very first step that requires capturing an image with the help of a digital camera.

Image Pre-processing

The images which are acquired cannot be directly fed into the CNN. Since, the computer does not understand anything other than binary numbers, the images also must be converted into machine understandable format.

Feature Extraction

After the images are processed into proper format and divided into segments, the next very important step is feature extraction. This step in CNN is done by using activation function followed by max-pooling layers.

Max-Pooling Layers

In CNN, usually every activation function layer is followed by a pooling layer. Pooling layers are used to reduce the dimensions of feature maps. They are 3 types of pooling average-pooling, max-pooling and global-pooling.

Classification

After the layers of the CNN are decided, programs finds out closest match to the captured image. And if the program identifies plant disease it provides information about the disease.

Result

On connecting the backend model to the frontend and deploying it on the server, the users can simply upload the pictures of the affected plant leaves and get the consultation for it at the click of a button.
